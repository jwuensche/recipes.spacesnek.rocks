# Basic yeast dough

> Usable for bread rolls üçû and pizza dough üçï, also sweets if that's your style

What you need:
- flour (wheat, spilt, whole, any kind really) around 1000 grams
- water around 400 ml
- yeast (dry or active)
- salt

Begin with mixing 200g of flour with the yeast, and heavy salt the mix (~30g). Pour water to the mix until everything is well homogenous, the mixture should be quite liquidy.
> Let it sit for around 10 min.
Then add a handful of flour and quirl until it's all one liquid again. Rinse and repeat as long as the dough sticks to the inside of your bowl.
The structure of the dough should be really soft, deform with minimal pressure, not sticky on the outside, but as soon as you're using more force on it's surface.
> Let it sit again for around one hour.
Get the dough out of the bowl, and knead with your hands until the dough get's a bit warmer. After that cut the dough into pieces if required and put it into the fridge for atleast 30 mins. After that it is ready to use.

> You can use the dough for about 2 days when you store it in the fridge.
